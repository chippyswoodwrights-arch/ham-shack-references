## Antenna Plan

### Phase 1 — Hustler Vertical on Roof (get on the air)
- **What:** Hustler HF vertical with resonator coils — multiband, one feedpoint
- **Mount:** Rooftop mast, shallow pitch roof
- **Radials:** Run on roof surface — manageable on shallow pitch
- **Bands:** 80m through 10m with appropriate resonators
- **Why first:** Simple, omnidirectional, no thinking required, gets him on all bands immediately
- **Good for:** Ragchewing, casual operating, learning the bands
- **Notes:** Well documented install, parts readily available, Hustler 4-BTV or 5-BTV
  are the common choices. Needs good radial system for performance.

### Phase 2 — EFHW Down the Slope (DX capability)
- **What:** End-fed half-wave wire antenna, 49:1 unun at feedpoint
- **Wire length:** ~130ft covers 80, 40, 20, 15, 10m harmonically
- **Directional bias:** Aimed east — Pacific, Asia, DX
- **Why second:** Leverages exceptional site geometry, low angle radiation off slope
- **Good for:** DX, digital modes, contesting, working rare entities
- **Cost:** Wire + unun, very low. Non-permanent install.
- **Notes:** Fetch primary source on EFHW design before building.
  This site is unusually well suited for this antenna type.

#### Confirmed EFHW Routing (worked out 2026-06-22)

**Feedpoint:** Garage/bonus room roof peak
- Unun mounts at the peak directly above the radio closet
- Coax drops 8-10ft straight down the blank west wall of the bonus room
- Penetrates closet wall — nearly zero feedline run, negligible loss
- This is the peak visible in street view with the dormer window below it

**Wire route:**
1. **Garage peak → SW oak** (Leg 1)
   - Large oak just off SW corner of house, rises above roofline
   - Wire gains 8-10ft+ of height from feedpoint to tree attachment
   - Sets up excellent downward slope angle toward east/south

2. **SW oak → down slope ~30ft** (Leg 2)
   - Steeply drops toward the adjacent lot
   - Low-angle radiation off the slope toward the Pacific — ideal for DX
   - Terminus at turn point on adjacent lot or pool deck approach

3. **Turn → pool deck railing** (Leg 3)
   - Wire runs along pool deck railing to reach full 130ft
   - Deck wraps around — provides the final length without extending onto adjacent lot
   - Attachment point: post or railing cleat at far end of deck

**Wire length math:**
- Garage peak → SW oak: ~30-40ft (horizontal + height gain)
- SW oak → turn point: ~30ft (steep drop, maybe more)
- Turn → pool deck extension: ~50-60ft along railing
- Total: ~110-130ft — should reach 130ft with railing run
- If short: add a few feet along the railing further north

**Height advantage:** Starting 8-10ft above roofline (via oak), then dropping steeply.
Rear of house is effectively 3 stories above back lot. Exceptional low-angle geometry.

#### Feedline Plan (non-permanent install)
- **Cable:** LMR-240 (~60-70ft)
- **Route:** Down west wall of bonus room → along roof surface to feedpoint at peak
- **Mount:** UV-resistant cable clips on roof surface — no penetrations, no permanent hardware
- **Rationale:** Matt is not keeping the property after Dad passes. Non-permanent is correct call.
  Roof run is short and low-visibility. Revisit only if long-term operation is planned.
- **Entry:** Penetrate closet wall directly (existing phone cable penetration in wrong corner —
  drill fresh hole in radio closet wall instead)

### Phase 3 — Beam (evaluate after 6-12 months operating)
- **Options:** Hex beam or small Yagi — site has room for either
- **Requirements:** Rotator, thrust bearing, mast, guy wires, serious mount
- **When:** Only if he gets serious about DX chasing and wants directional gain
- **Notes:** May require permits. Crew needed for safe installation.
  Don't start until Phase 1+2 are proven and operating style is understood.
